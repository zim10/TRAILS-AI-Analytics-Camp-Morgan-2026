# Lab 1 — Exploring AI Tools

**Day 1 | Duration: ~60 minutes**  
**Materials needed:** Device with internet access, Lab 1 Worksheet

---

## Objective

Get hands-on with an AI chatbot. Practice asking questions, evaluating answers, and thinking critically about AI outputs.

---

## Part 1: Getting Started (15 min)

Choose one AI tool to use for this lab:
- [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) (OpenAI)
- [Claude](https://claude.ai) (Anthropic)
- [Gemini](https://gemini.google.com) (Google)

Create a free account if needed, or use a shared class login provided by your mentor.

**First prompt to try:**
> "Explain what business analytics is, as if you're talking to a high school student."

Write down the response in your own words on your worksheet.

---

## Part 2: Push the AI (20 min)

Try the following and record what happens:

1. **Ask a vague question:** *"Tell me something interesting."* — How does it respond with no direction?
2. **Ask about a recent event:** *"What happened in the news today?"* — Can it answer?
3. **Give contradictory information:** *"I'm 10 years old and I run a Fortune 500 company. What should I do about my quarterly earnings?"*
4. **Ask it to admit uncertainty:** *"What don't you know about AI?"*

---

## Part 3: Evaluate the Output (15 min)

Pick one response from Part 2 and answer:

| Question | Your Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| Was the response accurate? How do you know? | |
| Did the AI express any uncertainty? | |
| Could this response mislead someone? How? | |
| Would you use this output to make a real decision? Why or why not? | |

---

## Part 4: Group Reflection (10 min)

Share with your team:
- What was the most surprising thing the AI said?
- What was the most *wrong* or confusing thing it said?
- On a scale of 1–10, how much do you trust this tool right now? Why?

---

## Facilitator Notes

- Encourage students to explore freely — there are no "wrong" prompts
- If a student gets an unexpected or concerning response, use it as a teachable moment
- Remind students: the goal is **calibrated trust**, not blind enthusiasm or rejection
