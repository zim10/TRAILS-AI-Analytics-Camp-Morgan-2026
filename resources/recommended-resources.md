# Recommended Resources

A curated list of accessible, high-quality resources for students, mentors, and facilitators.

---

## 🤖 Understanding AI (Beginner-Friendly)

- [Elements of AI](https://www.elementsofai.com/) — Free online course, no math required
- [AI for Everyone — Coursera (Andrew Ng)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/ai-for-everyone) — Non-technical intro
- [How does AI work? — MIT News explainer](https://news.mit.edu/2023/explained-generative-ai-1109)

---

## ⚖️ Trustworthy & Ethical AI

- [Algorithmic Justice League](https://www.ajl.org/) — Led by Joy Buolamwini; great videos on AI bias
- [AI Now Institute Reports](https://ainowinstitute.org/) — Policy-focused research on AI accountability
- [Coded Bias (Netflix Documentary)](https://www.codedbias.com/) — Highly recommended for Day 3

---

## 📊 Data & Business Analytics

- [Google Data Analytics Certificate (Coursera)](https://www.coursera.org/professional-certificates/google-data-analytics) — Beginner pathway
- [Tableau Public](https://public.tableau.com/) — Free data visualization tool
- [Our World in Data](https://ourworldindata.org/) — Real datasets for practice

---

## 💼 Careers in AI & Tech

- [Day of AI — MIT](https://dayofai.org/) — High school curriculum
- [Code.org](https://code.org/) — Intro programming for beginners
- [Bureau of Labor Statistics: Data Scientists outlook](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/math/data-scientists.htm)

---

## 📚 For Deeper Reading

- *Weapons of Math Destruction* by Cathy O'Neil
- *Atlas of AI* by Kate Crawford
- *The Alignment Problem* by Brian Christian
