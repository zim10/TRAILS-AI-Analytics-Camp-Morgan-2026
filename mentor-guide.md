## Welcome, Mentors!

Thank you for being part of Digital Futures. As an undergraduate or graduate student mentor, you are one of the most important parts of this program. High school students benefit enormously from near-peer mentorship — you represent what is possible for them.

---

## Your Role

You are **not** expected to be an AI expert. Your job is to:

- Be a **supportive presence** during labs and team work sessions
- Help students **stay on task** and work through confusion
- Share your **own college journey** authentically
- Facilitate **reflection conversations** at the end of each day
- Flag any concerns (student discomfort, confusion, conflict) to the faculty coordinator

---

## Lab Facilitation Tips

- Let students struggle productively — don't give answers immediately
- Ask guiding questions: *"What do you think is happening here?"* *"What would you change?"*
- Normalize confusion: *"This trips up a lot of people — let's figure it out together"*
- Keep energy positive, especially after lunch

---

## Discussion Facilitation

For end-of-day reflections, use these prompts if the group is quiet:
- *"What's one thing that surprised you today?"*
- *"Did anything today change how you think about AI?"*
- *"What question are you still sitting with?"*

Avoid leading questions that push students toward a particular opinion. The goal is **calibrated** thinking about AI — not enthusiasm or skepticism.

---

## Talking About Your Own Path

Students will ask you about college. Be honest and human:
- It's okay to share challenges, not just wins
- If you changed majors or weren't sure what you wanted — say so
- Connect your story to *why* digital literacy and tech skills matter

---

## Important Notes

- **Confidentiality:** Don't share student names or personal information outside the program
- **Inclusivity:** Be mindful of varying technical backgrounds — some students may have much more or less experience than others
- **Contact:** Reach out to the faculty coordinator immediately if a student seems distressed or a situation feels unclear
