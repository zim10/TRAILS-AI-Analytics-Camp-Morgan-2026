# Day 3 — Trustworthy AI: Ethics, Fairness & Accountability

**Theme:** How do we decide whether to trust an AI system?

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of Day 3, students will be able to:
- Define the five pillars of trustworthy AI
- Identify real-world examples of AI bias and its consequences
- Explain the difference between over-trust and under-trust in AI
- Apply a trustworthy AI lens to evaluate a system or decision

---

## The Five Pillars (Core Framework)

| Pillar | Key Question |
|--------|-------------|
| **Reliability/Accuracy** | Does the AI do what it claims to do, consistently? |
| **Fairness** | Does it treat all groups equitably? Who might be harmed? |
| **Privacy** | What data does it collect? How is it used and protected? |
| **Transparency/Explainability** | Can we understand why it made a decision? |
| **Accountability** | Who is responsible when it causes harm? |

## Lecture Outline (~70 min)

### Part 1: Why Trust Matters
- The difference between trust and blind faith
- Over-trust vs. under-trust: both are dangerous
- Real examples: predictive policing, hiring algorithms, medical diagnosis AI

### Part 2: Bias in AI
- Where bias comes from (training data, design choices, feedback loops)
- Disparate impact vs. disparate treatment
- Video case study: facial recognition bias (discuss as a class)

### Part 3: Who Is Accountable?
- Developers? Companies? Governments? Users?
- The concept of "algorithmic accountability"
- What does responsible AI deployment look like?

## Lab 3: Bias Detection Exercise

**Duration:** 60 minutes

### Scenario
Students are given a fictional AI hiring tool. The tool has been trained on historical hiring data.

### Tasks
1. Review the model's outputs for different demographic groups
2. Identify patterns that suggest bias
3. Propose one change to the training data or design that could reduce bias
4. Discuss: can you ever fully remove bias from an AI system?

### Reflection Questions
- Whose responsibility is it to catch bias — the company that built the AI, or the company that uses it?
- How would you feel if an AI decided whether you got a job or a scholarship?

---

## Case Study Discussion: When AI Goes Wrong

Teams review one of the following real-world cases and answer guiding questions:
- Amazon's scrapped AI recruiting tool
- COMPAS recidivism algorithm
- Apple Card gender bias allegations

**Guiding Questions:**
1. Which trustworthy AI pillar(s) were violated?
2. What were the real-world consequences?
3. What should have been done differently?

---

## Team Project Connection

Teams revisit their business problem and ask:
- Could an AI solution for this problem introduce bias or fairness concerns?
- What data would be needed, and could that data be biased?
- How would you explain the AI's decision to someone it affects?

Teams document their answers in their project notes — this will be part of their showcase rubric.
