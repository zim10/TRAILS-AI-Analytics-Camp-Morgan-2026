# 🛒 Supermarket Sales Dataset

A beginner-friendly dataset for learning **data analysis**. It contains **1,000 sales records** from a supermarket chain operating in three cities. Each row is one customer purchase.

Your job: explore the data, find interesting patterns, and present what you discover to the class!

---

## 📂 File
- `SuperMarket_Dataset.csv` — 1,000 rows, 8 columns, no missing values. 🎉

---

## 📊 Columns Explained

| # | Column | Type | What it means | Possible values |
|---|--------|------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **City** | Text | Store location | Yangon, Naypyitaw, Mandalay |
| 2 | **Customer type** | Text | Loyalty status | Member, Normal |
| 3 | **Gender** | Text | Customer gender | Female, Male |
| 4 | **Product line** | Text | Category of product bought | Health and beauty, Electronic accessories, Home and lifestyle, Sports and travel, Food and beverages, Fashion accessories |
| 5 | **Quantity** | Number | How many items were bought | 1 to 10 |
| 6 | **Sales** | Number | Total amount paid (in currency) | 10.68 to 1042.65 |
| 7 | **Payment** | Text | How the customer paid | Ewallet, Cash, Credit card |
| 8 | **Rating** | Number | Customer satisfaction score | 4.0 to 10.0 |

---

## 🧠 Key Terms
- **Categorical data** = labels/groups (City, Gender, Payment…)
- **Numerical data** = numbers you can do math on (Quantity, Sales, Rating)
- **Row / record** = one single purchase
- **Average (mean)** = add the values, divide by how many there are

---

## ❓ Questions to Get You Started
Pick a few of these — or invent your own!

1. Which **city** has the highest total sales?
2. Do **Members** spend more than **Normal** customers?
3. Which **product line** is the most popular? The most profitable?
4. Do **men or women** spend more on average?
5. What is the most common **payment method**?
6. Which product line gets the **best ratings**?
7. Is there a connection between **quantity bought** and **rating**?
8. What is the **average sale amount** overall?

---

## 🛠️ Tools You Can Use
- **Microsoft Excel / Google Sheets** — sort, filter, PivotTables, charts (easiest start)
- **Python, Tableau** (pandas + matplotlib) — for groups who want a challenge
- Any chart type: bar charts, pie charts, line graphs (use AI)

---

## ✅ Tips for Good Analysis
- Always start with a **question** before making a chart.
- Every chart needs a **title** and **labeled axes**.
- Don't just show numbers — explain **what they mean**.
- Compare groups (e.g., city vs. city) to find the interesting story.

Good luck, and have fun exploring the data! 🚀
